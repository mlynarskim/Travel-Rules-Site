Budget Road Trip: 5 Smart Ways to Save Money While Traveling

Travel Smarter, Not More Expensive

Many people give up on long road trips fearing the costs. The key is optimizing expenses wherever possible. Here are 5 rules to save your budget:

  • Fuel Up Off the Highway: Gas prices at highway stations can be up to 20% higher. Simply drive a few miles to the nearest town to save significant money on every tank.
  • Cook Your Own Meals: One restaurant meal for the whole family often equals the cost of fuel for 100-150 km. Investing in a good gas stove and shopping at local supermarkets is the easiest way to cut costs.
  • Choose Free Attractions: Instead of expensive theme parks or ticketed museums, opt for nature and public spaces. If you use iOS, the Travel Rules app helps you locate free spots nearby – from modern playgrounds and city parks to scenic viewpoints. It’s the perfect way to have an exciting day without spending a single euro.
  • Avoid Toll Roads: Highways in countries like France or Italy are very expensive. By setting your navigation to 'avoid tolls,' you’ll not only save money but also see much more interesting, scenic routes and local villages.
  • Free Drinking Water: Buying bottled water is an unnecessary expense and bad for the environment. Use public drinking fountains (very common in Southern Europe) or install a simple water filtration system in your car.
