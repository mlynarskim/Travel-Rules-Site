Weekend Flight: How to Pack a 3-Day Trip into a Small Carry-On

Minimalist travel – how to pack for 3 days

Low-cost airlines have completely changed the way people travel around Europe. A weekend trip to another country can sometimes cost less than dinner in a restaurant. The challenge usually appears during packing – many airlines allow only a small backpack that must fit under the seat.

The good news is that with the right packing strategy you can fit everything you need for a three-day trip. The key is minimalism, choosing versatile clothes and using a few proven packing tricks.

Check the baggage limits carefully

Every airline has slightly different rules regarding free carry-on luggage. In most cases it’s a small underseat backpack that must fit under the seat.

AirlineExample limit
Ryanair40 × 20 × 25 cm
Wizz Air40 × 30 × 20 cm
easyJet45 × 36 × 20 cm

The best strategy is to buy a backpack designed exactly for these dimensions. That way you avoid stress at the boarding gate.

The capsule wardrobe rule

The biggest packing mistake is bringing too many clothes. Instead, use the capsule wardrobe principle – every item should match the others.

Example outfit set for 3 days

  • 2 T-shirts
  • 1 light hoodie
  • 1 pair of pants
  • underwear for 3 days
  • light shoes or sneakers

The largest clothing items – such as a jacket or hoodie – are best worn during the flight.

Roll clothes instead of folding

One of the most effective ways to save space is rolling your clothes instead of folding them. This method:

  • takes up less space,
  • keeps the backpack organized,
  • reduces wrinkles.

You can also use small packing organizers or compression bags.

Toiletries – travel size versions

Carry-on luggage has strict liquid limits. All liquids must be in containers up to 100 ml and placed in a transparent bag.

How to reduce toiletries

  • use small travel bottles,
  • choose multi-purpose cosmetics,
  • check if your hotel offers basic toiletries.

In many cases you really need only a very small kit.

Use every bit of space

When packing, it’s worth using every empty space in your backpack.

  • put socks inside shoes,
  • store chargers in backpack pockets,
  • keep documents in a small pouch.

These small tricks can make a surprisingly big difference.

Packing checklist

During short trips it’s easy to forget small items like chargers or documents. That’s why preparing a packing checklist in advance can help a lot.

Travel apps can be useful when organizing such trips. For example, Travel Planner App allows you to create packing lists and plan a short trip step by step.

During the trip itself it can also be helpful to use apps that help find useful places in a new city – such as Travel Rules, which helps locate parks, rest areas and other practical locations.

Most common packing mistakes

  • bringing a backpack that is too large,
  • packing too many clothes,
  • large cosmetic products,
  • poor backpack organization.

Avoiding these mistakes allows you to travel extremely light.

Summary

Packing into a small carry-on bag is a skill that makes travel cheaper and more comfortable. With a few simple rules you can easily pack for a three-day trip and avoid paying for an additional suitcase.

The best part is that traveling with a small backpack makes you more mobile and spontaneous – exactly how short weekend adventures should feel.
