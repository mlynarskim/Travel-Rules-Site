Wild Camping vs. Campsites: Where to Sleep Safely and Legally in Europe?

Freedom or Safety? The Great Van Life Dilemma

For many, the essence of van life is wild camping – opening your sliding door to a view of the Alps without neighbors. However, legal realities in Europe are complex. Know the rules before you go.

1. Where is Wild Camping Legal?

  • Free Spirit Haven (Scandinavia, Estonia, Scotland): Thanks to 'Allemansrätten', you can sleep almost anywhere if you respect nature.
  • The Gray Zone (France, Germany, Spain): Officially restricted, but often tolerated for one night to 'restore the ability to drive'. Avoid setting up camping gear like tables or awnings.
  • Strict Bans (Croatia, Greece, Italy): Police are very active here, and fines can reach hundreds of euros.

2. When Is a Campsite Necessary?

Even the biggest fans of the wild need civilization. Campsites provide security, electricity, and the essential ability to legally empty your grey water tanks.

3. Tools for Smarter Planning

Use the Travel Planer App to track your accommodation costs and see how much you save by wild camping. For daytime recreation, use Travel Rules on iOS to find nearby free parks or playgrounds when your overnight spot lacks facilities.

4. Ethics – Leave No Trace

Always follow the 'Leave No Trace' rule. More places are closing for vans due to littering. Leave your spot cleaner than you found it!
