Document checklist before international travel: passport, visa, insurance

Passport — the first thing

Your passport must have at least 6 months validity from your departure date. This is not a recommendation — it's a requirement in most countries. Check expiration date 3 months before planned travel.

Visas — a lengthy process

Some countries require a visa, others don't. EU citizens can travel freely around Europe (no visa needed). Outside Europe? It depends.

Check requirements for your citizenship and destination on Travel Rules — the app has current visa requirements for every country.

Health insurance

EHIC (European Health Insurance Card) — free from your health service. Required for every trip to the EU.

For countries outside the EU — check if your insurance covers borders. Usually it doesn't.

Document copies

Always carry copies in a separate bag. If you lose your passport — recovery will be easier.

Summary

Document checklist is not complicated, but lack of preparation can ruin your entire trip. Make it 3 months ahead. Try Travel Rules — it has a built-in documents checklist.
